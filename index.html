Brianna Saylor

I am a student passionate about avian ecology and conservation, especially in understanding how birds use and respond to various habitats. My field experience includes techniques such as radio telemetry, mist netting, and vegetation sampling. Through my academic projects, I have developed skills in data analysis, R coding, and mapping, which have helped me explore patterns in species diversity and habitat relationships. This portfolio highlights my academic journey and research experience. Looking ahead, I hope to continue building on these experiences and contribute to effective conservation outcomes.

Field Experience

I have experience with the following field skills: point count surveys, radio telemetry, mist netting, vegetation surveys, autonomous recording unit maintenance, backroad navigation.

Research

The objective of my master's thesis was to identify the factors that influence the diversity of disturbance dependent species in Tennessee by 1) examining which habitat characteristics affect the overall richness of distrubance dependent species and 2) examining which habitat characteristics influence the occurrence of species of high conservation concern. I expect my research to be completed in 2026.

Data Wrangling and Analysis

My coursework has taught me a proficiency in coding and analyzing data with R. I have also coded in html and javascript. For analyses I have used ArcGIS and Google Earth Engine. Explore my work below.

Home Range Analysis

Using minimum convex polygon, kernel-density estimation, and continuous-time movement modeling to analyze home range.

Model Selection

Using general linear models to determine what factors impact bird detections during point counts.

Distribution Mapping

Using Google Earth Engine and climate modeling to predict a species occurrence probability.
